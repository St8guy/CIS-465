Implementation

From the examples that are provided below it can be taken away that the Otsu, and by extension the APT method, is effective at seperating the foreground and the background only under certain conditions. As seen with the coins image the fact that the background was black and that each of the objects was largely seperated. Even though they were different colors, dull colors though, the process accuratly seperated the coins from the background. Similarly the stand, clock, and shades in image1 was separated from the rest of the image effectivly due to similar reasons. A stark background with similar colors throughout the image. The issue arrises with the other two photos, the first of which image2 has a wide variaty of colors with a white background. While the background was properly excluded sections of the board itself were excluded as well due to different colors being mapped to very similar shades of grey. And with the lena image the fact that the background itself has a very similar color to that of the womans skin causes an issue. As the entirety of the photo has a very similar color pallet which leads to similar greys in the background and foreground. Resulting in sections of both the be excluded and included in various places. The APT process suffers from similar issues, but typically renders the forground objects more clearly. As the coins are whole in the result where as they originally had chunks missing from them in Otsu, or the clock being more readable from APT.
